Usability Overview — Physio

In this page, I am going to do a usability overview on tasks for physios, detailing:

  • Initial/current problems
  • Implemented solutions
  • Further enhancements/recommendations

The problems detailed here mostly arise from our user testing, but I will also do my own analysis by going through the app and some problems that I noticed while developing the frontend.

I will be recommending enhancements and solutions from both design and technical perspectives as the UI/UX Designer and Frontend Developer.

Recording Injuries

Problem

  • Initially, we did not have a list of injuries that the user could choose from that would pre-fill the injury details such as the injury name, type, recovery method, etc. so the physio would need to fill it in manually which would take time and cause multiple duplicating entries in the database.
  • We also did not have the feature where the physio could upload injury reports so the player couldn't access their detailed report and injury plan through our app.

Implemented Solutions

  • Having a list of the most common GAA injuries and letting the physio choose from them - Michael did a research on the most common GAA injuries and from that research, he populated our database with those injuries. After that I implemented this function in the frontend by making a dropdown populated with data about the injuries that the physio could choose from. I also implemented a search bar within the dropdown so that they can find the injury that they want with ease.
  • Implement uploading injury report PDF - We made changes in the database, backend, and frontend to accommodate uploading and viewing PDF in-app.

Further Enhancements

  • Our current solution is quite effective, yet it still poses a problem: what if the injury the player suffered is not included in our list? We shared a survey for physios asking for feedback regarding this and the majority of them would prefer if we have both options, choosing from a pre-filled dropdown and entering it manually, which is something that we can do in the future.
